Budge hall (pictured above) has been home to me, and 120 other residents this last school year.

I have had the priviledge to be a Resident Assistant, and provide some oversight to the large population of first-time college students.

This has been both educational and enlightening for me. In the left column is what I learned as an RA.

As an RA, you work largely with 18 year-olds living away from home for the first time

As such, there are always those who thing this new increase in independence exempts them from consequences, this is sometimes true, in the regards of eating chips in bed or not brushing your teeth.

However, other times, when they decide to take things a little too far, I, as an RA, get to deal with any repurcussions. In the right column are some of the incidents I have had to deal with this year.
